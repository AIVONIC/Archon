# Portuguese Property Market

## Market Overview (2026)

Portugal's property market continues to attract international buyers and investors across multiple regions. Year-over-year price growth approximately 9.3% (2025). Average rental yield 5.2-6.1%.

### Algarve

- Algarve average: EUR 3,200-3,870 per square meter
- Lagos municipality: EUR 4,500-5,700 per square meter
- Faro municipality: EUR 2,800-3,500 per square meter
- Tavira: EUR 3,000-4,200 per square meter
- Albufeira: EUR 3,200-4,800 per square meter
- Vila do Bispo / Aljezur (west coast): EUR 2,500-4,000 per square meter
- International buyer share: over 60% in coastal areas

### Lisbon

- City center: EUR 5,500-8,000 per square meter
- Greater Lisbon: EUR 3,200-5,000 per square meter
- Cascais / Estoril: EUR 4,500-7,000 per square meter

### Porto

- City center: EUR 3,500-5,500 per square meter
- Greater Porto: EUR 2,200-3,500 per square meter

### Silver Coast

- Obidos / Peniche / Nazare: EUR 2,000-3,500 per square meter
- Growing interest from Northern European buyers

### Alentejo

- Rural properties: EUR 800-2,000 per square meter
- Largest region, lowest density, increasing interest for lifestyle buyers

### Construction and Renovation Costs

- Light renovation: EUR 500-800 per square meter
- Medium renovation: EUR 800-1,500 per square meter
- Full renovation / reconstruction: EUR 1,500-2,500 per square meter
- New build: EUR 1,800-3,000 per square meter
- These are approximate ranges - actual costs depend on location, materials, scope, and contractor

## Western Algarve Specifics

The western Algarve (Lagos to Sagres) is structurally different from the central and eastern Algarve:

- Costa Vicentina Natural Park restricts new development, creating permanent supply constraints
- Hospitality permits are increasingly rare and difficult to obtain
- Existing permitted structures hold disproportionate value
- The area retains authentic village character with year-round communities
- Burgau, Luz, Salema, and other villages offer a lifestyle that the overdeveloped central Algarve cannot match

## Investment Context

Key factors for property investment in Portugal:

- Strong tourism demand (Algarve receives over 4 million visitors annually)
- Limited supply in western Algarve due to natural park protections
- Golden Visa program: still active but real estate investment route restricted in Lisbon and Porto, still available in interior and islands
- NHR (Non-Habitual Resident) tax regime: significant changes in recent years, professional tax advice essential
- AMI license required for professional property mediation
- Strong rental yields in prime coastal locations (5.2-6.1% average)
- Year-round climate supporting tourism beyond summer season
- Growing digital nomad and remote worker population driving rental demand
- Construction sector capacity constraints in popular areas
