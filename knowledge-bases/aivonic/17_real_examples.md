# Aivonic - How AI Agents Work by Industry

## E-Commerce

A customer visits your online store at 11pm on a Sunday. They want to know if a product is available in a different size. Your agent checks inventory, confirms availability, and offers to help them complete the purchase. Without the agent, that customer would have left and probably bought from a competitor.

What the agent handles: product questions, order status lookups, return and exchange processing, size and fit guidance, shipping information, payment processing, and after-hours sales assistance.

## Real Estate

A potential buyer browses property listings on your website. Your agent engages them, asks about their requirements (budget, location, number of bedrooms), matches them with suitable properties, and books a viewing appointment. The agent qualifies leads before they ever reach your inbox.

What the agent handles: property inquiries, viewing appointments, buyer qualification, neighborhood information, pricing questions, mortgage calculator guidance, and document requests.

## Professional Services (Law, Accounting, Consulting)

A potential client visits your firm's website wanting to know if you handle their type of case. Your agent asks qualifying questions, explains your services, collects their contact information, and schedules an initial consultation. Your team only speaks with pre-qualified prospects.

What the agent handles: service inquiries, client intake, appointment scheduling, document requests, fee structure questions, and case type qualification.

## Healthcare

A patient wants to book an appointment or ask about symptoms. Your agent handles scheduling, provides general health information from your knowledge base, answers questions about insurance acceptance, and triages urgent matters to your staff.

What the agent handles: appointment booking, office hours and location questions, insurance verification, prescription refill requests, post-visit follow-up, and general health FAQs.

## SaaS and Technology

A trial user is stuck on a feature. Your agent searches your documentation, provides step-by-step guidance, and if needed, escalates to your support team with full context. Onboarding questions are answered instantly, reducing churn.

What the agent handles: feature explanations, troubleshooting, onboarding guidance, pricing and plan comparisons, integration questions, and bug report collection.

## Hospitality and Restaurants

A guest wants to make a reservation, ask about the menu, or get directions. Your agent handles it all instantly in whatever language the guest speaks. Special dietary requirements are noted. Large party bookings are forwarded to your events team.

What the agent handles: reservations, menu questions, dietary accommodations, directions and parking, event inquiries, gift card purchases, and loyalty program information.

## Digital Agencies

You offer multiple services (web design, SEO, marketing) and prospects often do not know which they need. Your agent qualifies them by asking about their goals, budget, and timeline, then routes them to the right team member with a summary of the conversation.

What the agent handles: service explanations, project scoping, portfolio questions, timeline and pricing estimates, and meeting scheduling with the right specialist.

## Financial Services

Clients have questions about their accounts, investment options, or loan applications. Your agent provides account information, explains products, collects application details, and schedules advisor meetings. Compliance-sensitive topics are escalated to licensed professionals.

What the agent handles: account inquiries, product explanations, application intake, advisor scheduling, market information from your knowledge base, and document requests.
