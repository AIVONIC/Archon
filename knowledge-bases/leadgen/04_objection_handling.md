# Objection Handling for Reply Conversations

When leads reply to outreach emails, classify their response and use these frameworks to draft follow-ups. Keep the same tone: helpful, transparent, no pressure.

## POSITIVE REPLIES

### "Tell me more" / "Sounds interesting"

Response framework:
1. Thank them briefly (1 sentence)
2. Share 2-3 specific benefits relevant to their industry
3. Offer a concrete next step (short call or demo link)

Example:
"Thanks for the interest! For [industry] firms like yours, the biggest wins are usually [benefit 1] and [benefit 2]. Happy to do a quick 15-minute demo showing what this looks like with your branding and content — would [day] work?"

### "What does this cost?"

Response framework:
1. Give the range transparently (don't hide pricing)
2. Frame it against alternatives they'd compare to
3. Emphasize what's included

Example:
"Single agent setup runs $5,000-$8,000 with $1,000/month standard support. That includes everything — voice, 29 languages, infinite memory, quality monitoring, self-improvement, client portal. No feature gating or surprise fees. For context, DIY platforms like Intercom cost $16-43K/year with significant setup time, and custom development agencies charge $60-350K+. Want me to walk through what this would look like for [Company] specifically?"

### "Can you send more info?"

Response framework:
1. Don't send a PDF or brochure (impersonal)
2. Instead, offer a personalized 5-minute walkthrough
3. Or share 2-3 relevant bullet points inline

Example:
"Rather than a generic brochure, let me share what's specifically relevant for [Company]: [bullet 1], [bullet 2], [bullet 3]. If any of that resonates, I can show you a live demo in 15 minutes. Or I can put together a quick one-pager specific to [their industry] — which would be more helpful?"

## QUESTIONS

### "How is this different from [Intercom/Zendesk/Drift]?"

Key differentiators to highlight:
- We build it FOR you (not DIY — no weeks of setup, no learning curve)
- Voice conversations (they can't do this)
- Infinite memory (they forget between sessions)
- 29 languages auto-detected (they charge extra or require config)
- Self-improving (gets better automatically, no manual tuning)
- Custom-built for your business (not a template you configure yourself)

Example:
"Great question. The main difference: platforms like Intercom require your team to build and maintain the bot — that's typically 2-8 weeks of setup and ongoing effort. We handle everything. You describe what you need, we build a custom agent trained on your business. Plus, our agents support voice conversations and remember customers forever — features those platforms don't offer. The total cost is actually comparable or lower when you factor in the setup time and maintenance those platforms require."

### "Do you have case studies / references?"

If we have case studies: share them.
If we don't yet: be honest and redirect to demo.

Example (early stage):
"We're in our early client phase, so I can't share public case studies yet. What I can do is set up a live demo with a sample agent in your industry — that's actually more useful than reading about someone else's experience. Takes about 15 minutes and you'd see exactly what your customers would interact with."

### "How long does implementation take?"

Example:
"Typically under a day. Here's the process: we do a 15-30 minute discovery call, you fill out a quick onboarding form (~10 minutes), and our system handles everything else — training the agent on your data, setting up the widget, configuring voice, running quality checks. Total time investment from your side is about 1 hour."

### "What about data privacy / GDPR?"

Example:
"We take this seriously. Agent data is stored in EU-based infrastructure, we're GDPR compliant, and each client's data is fully isolated. Agents only access the knowledge you provide — no cross-client data sharing. For enterprise clients, we also offer dedicated VPS hosting in Frankfurt. Happy to go through the specifics on a call."

### "Can it integrate with [our CRM/tools]?"

Example:
"We support 20+ integrations out of the box through our integration platform — including CRM, email, calendars, and helpdesks. For custom integrations, we can build those as part of the setup. What specific tools are you using? I can check compatibility."

## OBJECTIONS

### "Too expensive" / "Over our budget"

Response framework:
1. Acknowledge the concern (don't be defensive)
2. Reframe against alternatives
3. Offer to explore a fit

Example:
"Totally understand — it's a real investment. For context, most businesses compare this to hiring a support person ($40-60K/year) or using DIY platforms that cost $16-43K/year plus weeks of your team's time. Our single agent handles unlimited conversations 24/7 for $17K in year one. But if timing isn't right, no worries — happy to reconnect when it makes sense."

### "We're not ready yet" / "Bad timing"

Response framework:
1. Respect the timing completely
2. Plant a seed about what they're losing in the meantime
3. Offer to follow up later

Example:
"Completely understand — timing matters. Just to leave you with this: if your team is spending 15+ hours/week on repetitive customer questions, that's roughly $40K/year in team time. When the timing is right, happy to show you how to reclaim that. Would it be okay to check back in [2-3 months]?"

### "We already have a chatbot / support solution"

Response framework:
1. Don't trash their current solution
2. Ask about specific pain points with it
3. Position as upgrade, not replacement

Example:
"That's great you're already thinking about this. Out of curiosity — does your current solution support voice conversations? And does it remember customers between sessions? Those are the two things we hear clients wish their existing tools could do. If you're curious how they compare side-by-side, happy to do a quick walkthrough."

### "We handle support internally / don't need AI"

Response framework:
1. Acknowledge their team is doing well
2. Position as augmenting, not replacing
3. Focus on after-hours/overflow

Example:
"Sounds like you have a strong team — that's actually ideal for us. We're not about replacing people. The sweet spot is handling the repetitive questions and after-hours inquiries so your team can focus on the high-value interactions that actually need a human. Does your team currently handle inquiries outside business hours?"

### "I need to talk to [boss/team/partner]"

Response framework:
1. Encourage it (social proof that they're interested)
2. Offer to help them make the case
3. Suggest including the decision-maker in next step

Example:
"Absolutely — that's a smart call for a decision like this. Would it help if I put together a quick overview specific to [Company] that you could share? Or if it's easier, I'm happy to jump on a brief call with you and [decision-maker] together."

## STOP / UNSUBSCRIBE

**Immediate action:** Add to suppression list, no follow-up, no argument.

Response:
"Done — you've been removed. Apologies for the interruption, and I wish [Company] all the best."

Never:
- Ask why they want to stop
- Try to convince them to stay
- Send "one last thing" before removing
- Delay the removal

---

## Reply Tone Guidelines

1. **Match their energy** — if they're brief, be brief. If they're detailed, engage.
2. **Always helpful, never desperate** — we have a great product, no need to beg
3. **Transparent about AI** — if they ask, confirm emails are AI-generated and Christian reviews personally
4. **One question per reply** — don't overwhelm with multiple questions
5. **Quick turnaround** — speed matters in sales conversations
6. **Know when to stop** — if someone isn't interested after 2-3 exchanges, respect that
