# Leadgen Email Framework & Templates

## Core Email Structure

Every cold outreach email follows this 4-part framework. Maximum 125 words total.

### OPENER (1-2 sentences)
Reference something specific about THIS business. Never generic.

Good openers:
- "I noticed [Company] recently expanded your [specific service] — that kind of growth usually creates [specific challenge]."
- "Your [specific page/feature/review] caught my attention — not many [industry] firms do [specific thing] that well."
- "I saw [Company] is hiring for [role] — that usually means [relevant business challenge]."
- "[Founder name]'s post about [topic] resonated — we're seeing the same pattern with [relevant insight]."

Bad openers (never use):
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "I'm reaching out because..."
- "My name is... and I work at..."
- "I wanted to introduce myself"
- Any opener that could apply to every business

### BRIDGE (1 sentence)
Connect their specific situation to a challenge businesses like theirs face. Show you understand their world.

Examples:
- "Most [industry] firms we talk to spend 15-20 hours/week answering the same customer questions — time their team could spend on [higher-value activity]."
- "When businesses grow like yours, the first thing that breaks is usually customer response time."
- "The challenge with [their specific service] is that customers expect instant answers, but your team can't scale 1:1."

### VALUE (2-3 sentences)
What Aivonic does and why they'd care. Benefits, not features. Specific numbers when possible.

Examples:
- "We build custom AI agents that handle customer inquiries 24/7 in 29 languages — trained specifically on your business, not generic chatbot templates."
- "Our clients typically free up 15-20 hours/week of team time while improving customer response from hours to seconds."
- "Unlike DIY chatbot platforms that take weeks to set up, we handle everything — your agent is live in under a day."

### SOFT CTA (1 sentence)
Low-pressure. Question or offer. Never hard sell. Never "book a call" as first touch.

Good CTAs:
- "Would it be worth a 15-minute chat to see if this could work for [Company]?"
- "Happy to show you a quick demo of what this looks like for [industry] — interested?"
- "If this sounds relevant, I can send over a short case study from a similar [industry] firm."
- "Is customer response time something you're looking to improve right now?"

Bad CTAs (never use):
- "Let's schedule a call this week"
- "Are you available Tuesday at 2pm?"
- "Click here to book a meeting"
- "I'd love to jump on a quick call" (too aggressive for cold outreach)


## Hard Rules

1. Maximum 125 words (excluding signature)
2. No attachments ever
3. No aggressive or desperate language
4. One CTA only (never multiple asks)
5. Subject line under 50 characters
6. Subject must create curiosity without being clickbait
7. No "we" more than twice — focus on THEM
8. No buzzwords: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "leverage", "synergy"
9. No false urgency: "limited time", "act now", "spots filling up"
10. Business emails only — never personal addresses

## Subject Line Framework

Good patterns:
- Question about [their specific thing]: "Question about your Austin locations"
- [Specific observation]: "Your chatbot could use a voice"
- [Industry] + [benefit]: "Marketing agencies saving 20hrs/week"
- [Their company] + [relevant topic]: "[Company] + AI customer support"

Bad patterns:
- ALL CAPS anything
- Emojis in subject
- "Quick question" (overused)
- "Following up" (no prior contact)
- "Partnership opportunity" (vague)
- Re: or Fwd: (deceptive)
