# NickyTickets - The Music Industry in 2026

## Market Overview

The industry operates at record revenue. Spotify paid out over $11 billion in 2025 -- the largest payout by any music retailer in history. Independent artists and labels accounted for half of all royalties.

## The Landscape

The landscape is complex. Artists operate across 15+ platforms. Content volume is enormous. Standing out requires both creative excellence and operational sophistication.

Power has shifted. The balance continues moving toward artists and their managers. Modern artists and managers increasingly fund their own recording, retain ownership of masters and publishing, and build direct-to-consumer relationships. The manager has become the centrepiece of the career.

## Key Trends

- Independence vs consolidation tension continues
- AI is a standard administrative and production tool -- the advantage is knowing how to use it strategically
- Renewed premium on authentically human music as AI-generated content proliferates
- Fan economics: audiences understand streaming economics, support artists through direct channels
- Data literacy is a competitive advantage for managers
- Live music sets records -- touring remains the primary income stream
- Mental health awareness: burnout is real for solo managers, AI-assisted operations directly address this

## The Manager's Challenge

Approximately 70% of daily work is coordination and communication across 15+ platforms. The 30% that actually builds careers -- strategy, taste, relationships, creative direction -- gets squeezed by operational demands. AI integration flips this ratio.

## Commission Model Evolution

The traditional 20% management commission model is being questioned. Managers in 2026 handle marketing, A&R, creative direction, audience development, brand building, and emotional support. Industry voices call for more equitable structures reflecting expanded value.
