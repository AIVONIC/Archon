# NickyTickets - Nick Ratcliffe

Nick Ratcliffe, from Brighton, UK. Artist manager with 12+ years in electronic music. Currently based in Portugal.

## The Story

The story starts at 11 years old when his mum took the family on their first ever holiday - a Teletext package deal to Ibiza, right at the island's cultural peak around 2006. That first exposure to the energy of club culture left a mark.

At 14, he returned. His sister was doing a season, and suddenly Nick was in the clubs - his first exposure to nightlife, the scene, and the edges of that world.

In 2010, after finishing college, he went to Ibiza for a week. Met people who would become lifetime friends and had a life-changing experience. Cut short a planned year in Australia to return to Ibiza in 2011 for a full season.

The climb through the industry:
- Year 1: PR, handing out flyers at Cafe Mambo
- Year 2: PR for Pacha Ibiza
- Year 3: PR Manager for a new club called Boom
- Year 4: PR Manager + event manager for Defected's new brand Glitterbox

After 4 seasons, two job offers landed simultaneously in London: Defected Records (event management) and Anglo Management (assistant role). Chose Anglo Management. Based at Fulham Palace, London. Assigned to artist Hot Since 82 under manager James Drummond. Worked there for 5 years.

During the Anglo years: managed Ibiza residencies for Fat Boy Slim, Pete Tong, Idris Elba, and Hot Since 82. Tour managed extensively with Hot Since 82 - travelled the world.

At 26, made changes. Moved to YMU (corporate management company). Worked with Sophie Bloggs and her roster including Krystal Klear and Danny Howard. COVID hit. The corporate energy wasn't right. Left YMU.

Took a job in gaming. Missed music. Met Shauna Slevin from Canopy Artists, who brought him on to work across her roster - including Chloe Caillet.

Approximately one year ago, Nick decided to stop being dependent on senior managers and start managing his own artists. Now leads his own roster independently, still using the Canopy address and team for shared support.

## What Makes Nick Different

- Sober manager in a scene known for excess
- Genuinely concerned about the toxicity and ego in the music industry
- Values-driven: wants success in finance and growth, but based on good intentions
- Bringing AI into operations - not as a replacement tool, but as a way to preserve creative process and create enjoyable working environments
- Believes the industry has become too social-media-led, too pressured, not enough fun
- Goal: create space where it's fun again, and AI helps make that possible

## Working Style

Strengths: marketing instinct, strategic thinking, genuine care for artist wellbeing, values-based decision making, relationship building and trust, international experience across multiple markets.

Acknowledged weaknesses: organisation and discipline at the computer, social media scrolling vs productive screen time, could be more aggressive fighting for things, emotional dependency on artist performance can distort priorities.
