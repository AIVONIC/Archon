## Case Study 1: European Tour Optimisation

**The situation:** An electronic act with strong streaming numbers but inconsistent live bookings. Playing the same markets repeatedly, leaving significant audiences unserved. Tour routing was reactive rather than strategic.

**What we did:** Analysed geographic streaming data across Spotify for Artists and Apple Music to identify underserved markets where audience existed but no bookings had been made. Cross-referenced with BandsInTown demand data and promoter networks. Rebuilt the touring strategy around data-informed route planning, targeting markets where the audience was waiting rather than markets where the artist was already known.

**The outcome:** Expanded the touring footprint into 6 new markets within the first year. Average ticket sales increased because the artist was playing to hungry audiences, not oversaturated ones. Reduced dead legs in tour routing by 40%, cutting travel costs significantly. The relationship with three new promoters became recurring bookings.

**What made it work:** The AI analytics agent identified the geographic opportunity from streaming data. The human manager knew which promoters to call and how to pitch an unproven market. Data opened the door, relationships walked through it.

## Case Study 2: Release Campaign with AI-Powered Timing

**The situation:** An emerging artist ready to release a debut EP. No established release history to build on. Limited marketing budget. Needed maximum impact from a single campaign.

**What we did:** Used the analytics agent to study release timing patterns -- when the artist's audience was most active across DSPs, when editorial playlist turnover happens, when competing releases in the same genre were scheduled. Identified an optimal release window that avoided major label drops in the same genre space. Built the campaign around a 10-week cycle: pre-save campaign, social content series, targeted Meta ads in the artist's top 5 streaming markets, direct outreach to playlist curators and press.

**The outcome:** EP entered Spotify Release Radar and Discover Weekly within the first week. Two tracks picked up by independent playlist curators with a combined reach of 500k+ followers. Social media following grew 35% during the campaign period. Most importantly, the save-to-stream ratio held above 6% -- indicating the audience gained was engaged, not just passing traffic.

**What made it work:** The AI agent processed data from multiple platforms to find the timing window. The manager's industry relationships got the music in front of the right curators. The combination of data-informed strategy and human outreach outperformed what either could achieve alone.

## Case Study 3: Crisis Management -- Tour Disruption

**The situation:** Mid-tour, a key festival booking cancelled 72 hours before the show due to weather/permit issues. The artist was already in transit. Hotel, flights, and crew were booked and non-refundable for those dates. A gap in the tour schedule meant lost income and stranded team.

**What we did:** Within 4 hours of the cancellation: contacted promoters in three nearby cities using existing relationships, secured a replacement club booking for the same night, rerouted travel arrangements, negotiated a partial refund on the original hotel booking, updated all tour platforms (BandsInTown, SongKick, Spotify, website), and ran a flash social media campaign promoting the replacement show.

**The outcome:** Replacement show sold to 80% capacity on 48 hours notice. The artist stayed on the road, the crew stayed employed, and the tour P&L stayed positive. The flash social campaign generated more engagement than the original festival promotion.

**What made it work:** Speed and relationships. The AI communication agent handled the platform updates and social scheduling in parallel while the manager worked the phones. What would have taken one person a full day was handled in hours.
