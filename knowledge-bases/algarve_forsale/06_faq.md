## Q: Can foreigners buy property in Portugal?

Yes, with no restrictions. Foreign buyers have the same ownership rights as Portuguese citizens. You'll need a NIF (tax number) and a Portuguese bank account, and we strongly recommend working with a local lawyer.

## Q: What taxes will I pay?

The main purchase tax is IMT (Municipal Transfer Tax), plus 0.8% stamp duty, notary fees, and legal fees. The exact IMT amount depends on the property classification and value. A lawyer can provide precise calculations.

## Q: Is there a mortgage available?

Foreign buyers can typically obtain mortgages from Portuguese banks with 60-70% loan-to-value ratios. However, commercial property mortgages may have different terms. A mortgage broker can advise on current options.

## Q: What about the Golden Visa?

The Golden Visa program no longer accepts standard property purchases for residency. Property ownership alone doesn't grant residency rights. There are alternative visa routes available -- a D7 visa or Digital Nomad visa may be relevant depending on your situation.

## Q: How much would development cost?

This varies enormously depending on the concept and specification. For a property of this scale, development investment would be substantial. We recommend engaging a local architect and construction firm for estimates based on your specific plans.

## Q: Can I visit the property?

Absolutely. We can arrange a private viewing at your convenience. The property is fully accessible in its current state.

## Q: What about water supply?

The property benefits from natural water abundance -- a significant advantage in the Algarve, where water scarcity is a growing concern and can constrain development.
