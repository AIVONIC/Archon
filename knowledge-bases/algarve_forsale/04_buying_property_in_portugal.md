# Algarve ForSale - Buying Property in Portugal

## Can Foreigners Buy?

Yes. Portugal has no restrictions on foreign property ownership. EU citizens, non-EU citizens, and companies can all purchase property with the same rights as Portuguese citizens. No special permits are required. The process is transparent and well-regulated.

## The Buying Process (Step by Step)

1. Obtain a NIF (Numero de Identificacao Fiscal): Portuguese tax identification number, required for all financial transactions. Can be obtained at any Financas (tax office) or through a lawyer. Non-EU citizens previously needed a fiscal representative, though this requirement has been relaxed for EU citizens.

2. Open a Portuguese bank account: Not legally required but highly practical for handling transactions, paying taxes, and managing ongoing costs.

3. Property search and viewing: View the property, assess its condition, and understand the local market.

4. Make an offer: Offers are typically made verbally or in writing through the agent. There is no formal offer process like in some countries.

5. Reservation deposit: Typically EUR 5,000-10,000 to take the property off the market while contracts are prepared. Usually held in escrow by the lawyer or agency.

6. Promissory Contract (CPCV -- Contrato de Promessa de Compra e Venda): The legally binding contract that commits both buyer and seller. Includes price, deposit amount, completion date, and any conditions. Buyer typically pays 10-30% deposit at this stage. Under Portuguese law: if the buyer withdraws without cause, they forfeit the deposit; if the seller withdraws, they must return double the deposit.

7. Due diligence: Lawyer verifies legal ownership (certidao permanente), checks for liens or encumbrances, confirms building permits, habitation licences, energy certificate, and planning compliance. For this property specifically, verification of the existing build permit status is critical.

8. Deed signing (Escritura Publica): The final transfer of ownership, signed before a notary or at a Casa Pronta office. Buyer pays the remaining balance. Notary verifies all documents.

9. Registration: Property registered in the buyer's name at the Land Registry (Conservatoria do Registo Predial) and with the Tax Authority (Financas).

## Typical Timeline

From accepted offer to completion: approximately 4-12 weeks, though this can stretch longer with mortgage approvals or complex title issues.

## Taxes and Costs

- IMT (Municipal Transfer Tax): Progressive rate from 0% to approximately 8% depending on property value and type. For commercial/industrial property the rates may differ from residential. At EUR 900,000, expect to budget significant IMT costs -- exact amount depends on classification.
- Stamp Duty (Imposto de Selo): 0.8% of the purchase price
- Notary and registration fees: Typically EUR 500-1,500
- Legal fees: Typically 1-2% of purchase price
- Ongoing: IMI (annual property tax), typically 0.3-0.545% for urban properties

## Important Notes for This Property

- This is an industrial/commercial structure, not a standard residential property. Tax classification and permit processes may differ.
- The existing build permit for spa/restaurant/event space should be verified for current validity and any conditions.
- Development costs to convert from shell to operational facility will be substantial and should be budgeted separately from the purchase price.
- Construction costs in the Algarve in 2025-2026 vary significantly based on specification, but buyers should budget for substantial fit-out investment.
- Engaging a Portuguese lawyer experienced in commercial property is strongly recommended.

## Mortgages for Foreign Buyers

Non-resident buyers typically receive loan-to-value ratios between 60-70%, requiring 30-40% down payment. Mortgage terms up to 25 years for non-residents. Interest rates have been falling, with average new rates under 4% in 2025. Many banks offer mixed-rate mortgages with initial fixed periods.

Note: A mortgage on this type of commercial/industrial property may have different terms or requirements than standard residential mortgages. Some banks may require additional documentation or offer less favourable terms for commercial assets.

## Golden Visa

The Portuguese Golden Visa program no longer includes standard residential real estate investment as an eligible route (as of October 2023). Property purchase alone does not grant residency. Alternative residency routes include the D7 Visa (passive income/retirement), Digital Nomad Visa, and investment in qualifying funds.
