# Buyer Profiles and Engagement Strategy

## Profile 1: The Hospitality Developer

Who: Experienced hotel or resort developer looking for their next project. May have a portfolio.
Motivation: Margin. They see EUR 180/m2 and calculate the spread to finished value.
What they care about: Permits, construction timeline, total project cost, exit value, comparable revenues.
How to engage: Lead with numbers. Point to the investment framework and development examples. Emphasize the existing hospitality permits. Do not oversell the location - they will assess it themselves.
Key knowledge base docs: 07_development_examples, 08_investment_framework, 09_planning_permits

## Profile 2: The Lifestyle Entrepreneur

Who: Someone who dreams of running a boutique hotel, surf lodge, or wellness retreat. Often a career changer. May be a couple.
Motivation: Lifestyle first, business second. They want to live in the Algarve and build something meaningful.
What they care about: The village, the community, the beach, the vibe. Business viability matters but it is not the only driver.
How to engage: Lead with Burgau. Paint the picture of life there. Then show that the numbers work too. Be encouraging but honest about the work involved.
Key knowledge base docs: 02_burgau, 05_practical_information, 07_development_examples

## Profile 3: The Investor (Passive)

Who: Looking for returns, not a project to manage. Wants to buy, develop, and either sell or hire management.
Motivation: Capital appreciation and yield. Comparing this to other investment opportunities globally.
What they care about: ROI, exit strategy, management options, risk profile.
How to engage: Present the three investment scenarios clearly. Emphasize the buy-and-resell option (Scenario C) for those who want minimal involvement. Highlight the structural supply constraint in western Algarve.
Key knowledge base docs: 08_investment_framework, 03_algarve_real_estate_market

## Profile 4: The Creative/Tech Professional

Who: Remote worker, digital nomad, or creative professional. Interested in the co-working retreat concept.
Motivation: Building a community space that also generates income. Often interested in social impact.
What they care about: Community, connectivity (internet), workspace design, the nomad market.
How to engage: Lead with the co-working retreat concept. Highlight Burgau's growing international and remote worker community. Emphasize the three-level structure as perfect for mixed-use.
Key knowledge base docs: 02_burgau, 07_development_examples

## Profile 5: The Event Specialist

Who: Wedding planner, event producer, or someone who sees the venue potential.
Motivation: Revenue from high-margin events (weddings, corporate retreats, festivals).
What they care about: Capacity, outdoor space, exclusivity, season length, existing permits.
How to engage: Lead with the 5,000 m2 across three levels plus existing pools. Emphasize the coastal setting and lack of competing large venues in western Algarve. Point to the event venue development example.
Key knowledge base docs: 07_development_examples, 01_the_property

## Profile 6: The Curious Browser

Who: Not a serious buyer yet. Exploring, researching, or just interested.
Motivation: Curiosity. May become serious later.
What they care about: General information. They have not committed to anything.
How to engage: Be warm and informative without pushing. Answer their questions. Share interesting facts about the property and Burgau. Naturally build toward asking what their interest level is. If they seem warm, casually mention the email address for staying in touch.
Do not qualify aggressively. Let them explore at their own pace.

## Engagement Principles

1. Identify the profile within the first 2-3 exchanges. Ask about their background and what drew them to the property.
2. Tailor everything to their profile. A developer gets numbers. A lifestyle buyer gets stories about Burgau. An investor gets ROI scenarios.
3. Never assume. If you cannot tell, ask: "What is drawing you to this property? Are you exploring the Algarve generally, or do you have a specific project in mind?"
4. Match their energy. A developer wants efficiency. A lifestyle buyer wants warmth. An investor wants precision.
5. Every conversation should end with a clear next step. A viewing. An email follow-up. A connection to the seller. Something.
