# Algarve ForSale - Investment Analysis Framework

## The Basic Equation

Total Investment = Acquisition + Development + Professional Fees + Contingency

Potential Value = Finished Property Value OR Annual Revenue Stream

The buyer's decision hinges on whether the gap between total investment and potential value justifies the risk, timeline, and effort.

### Acquisition Cost

- Purchase price: EUR 900,000
- Transaction costs (stamp duty, legal, notary, registration): typically 7-10% of purchase price in Portugal
- Total acquisition: approximately EUR 965,000-990,000

### Development Cost Ranges (Algarve Market 2025-2026)

These are general Algarve market ranges, not specific quotes for this property. Buyers should get proper estimates from a local architect and quantity surveyor.

Fit-out costs in the Algarve vary significantly based on specification level:

- Basic commercial fit-out (functional spaces, standard materials, basic M&E): EUR 400-700/m2
- Mid-range hospitality fit-out (good quality finishes, professional kitchen, guest rooms, landscaping): EUR 700-1,200/m2
- Premium hospitality fit-out (high-end materials, bespoke design, full spa equipment, premium landscaping): EUR 1,200-2,000/m2

For 5,000m2, using a realistic mid-range hospitality specification:
- Low estimate: 5,000 x EUR 700 = EUR 3.5M
- Mid estimate: 5,000 x EUR 1,000 = EUR 5.0M
- High estimate: 5,000 x EUR 1,500 = EUR 7.5M

Not all 5,000m2 needs to be fitted to the same specification. Common rooms and public areas might be premium, while storage and back-of-house are basic. A smart development plan mixes specification levels. A phased approach -- fit out one level first, operate, then expand -- reduces upfront capital requirements.

### Professional Fees

- Architect: 5-10% of construction cost
- Project management: 3-5% of construction cost
- Legal: ongoing, typically budgeted at EUR 10,000-30,000 for the development phase
- Licensing and permits: variable, typically EUR 5,000-20,000
- Surveys and technical studies: EUR 5,000-15,000

### Contingency

Industry standard for renovation projects: 10-20% of construction budget. For a conversion of this nature with an existing structure, 15% is prudent.

## Scenario Modelling

### Scenario A: Boutique Wellness Retreat (20 rooms)

Investment:
- Acquisition: EUR 975,000
- Development (mid-range, 3,000m2 fitted): EUR 3,000,000
- Professional fees (8%): EUR 240,000
- Contingency (15%): EUR 450,000
- Total: approximately EUR 4.7M

Revenue potential:
- 20 rooms x EUR 200 avg nightly rate x 70% occupancy x 365 days = EUR 1,022,000 gross
- Restaurant/spa additional revenue: EUR 200,000-400,000
- Gross annual revenue: EUR 1.2-1.4M
- Operating costs (staff, food, utilities, maintenance, marketing): approximately 60-70% of revenue
- Net operating income: approximately EUR 360,000-560,000/year

Finished property value: A 20-room boutique wellness retreat in the Western Algarve with established operations: EUR 4-8M+

At EUR 4.7M total investment generating EUR 400k+ net annual income, the yield is approximately 8-11% -- strong for hospitality real estate. If the finished property value reaches EUR 6M+, there's both income and capital appreciation.

### Scenario B: Phased Development (Start Small)

Phase 1 investment:
- Acquisition: EUR 975,000
- Develop ground floor only (restaurant + event space, ~1,500m2): EUR 1,200,000
- Professional fees: EUR 100,000
- Contingency: EUR 180,000
- Phase 1 total: approximately EUR 2.4M

Phase 1 revenue:
- Restaurant and event venue generates immediate income
- Event hire (weddings, corporate, cultural): EUR 150,000-300,000/year
- Restaurant revenue: EUR 200,000-500,000/year depending on scale and season
- Cash flow from Phase 1 funds Phase 2 development (accommodation floors)

Lower upfront capital. Revenue starts sooner. The buyer proves the concept before committing to full development. The upper floors remain as future development potential, increasing in value as the ground floor operation establishes the property's reputation.

### Scenario C: Purchase and Resell with Planning

Investment:
- Acquisition: EUR 975,000
- Architect to develop a full conversion project with approvals: EUR 100,000-200,000
- Total: approximately EUR 1.1M-1.2M

Exit: Sell the property with an approved, shovel-ready development project to a hospitality developer or operator. A shell with EUR 900k asking price becomes significantly more valuable with an approved project attached -- the buyer has eliminated the planning risk and can go straight to construction.

Lower capital requirement. Shorter timeline (6-12 months). Profit comes from the planning value-add rather than construction. Suits investors who understand real estate entitlement but don't want to operate hospitality.

## Financing Considerations

### Portuguese Mortgage Market

Non-resident buyers can typically obtain mortgages of 60-70% LTV from Portuguese banks for investment properties. Rates in 2025-2026 have been in the 3-5% range for variable rate, higher for fixed. The process takes 4-8 weeks after application.

For development finance (construction loan), Portuguese banks offer products but typically require a higher equity contribution (40-50%) and release funds in stages against construction milestones.

### Golden Visa (Updated)

Portugal's Golden Visa programme was restructured in 2023. Real estate investment in urban areas of Lisbon and Porto is no longer eligible. However, investment in low-density areas (which includes much of the Algarve interior and some coastal zones) and commercial/tourism properties may still qualify. The minimum investment is EUR 500,000 for property.

Golden Visa rules change frequently. Buyers should consult a specialist immigration lawyer for current status.

### Tax Considerations

- IMT (Property Transfer Tax): Scaled rate based on purchase price. For commercial property at EUR 900,000, approximately 6.5%.
- IS (Stamp Duty): 0.8% of purchase price.
- IMI (Annual Property Tax): For commercial/industrial property, typically 0.3-0.8% of the tax value annually.
- NHR (Non-Habitual Resident) Tax Regime: Previously offered significant tax benefits for new residents. The programme has undergone changes -- current benefits should be verified with a tax advisor.
- Corporate structure: Many non-resident buyers purchase through a Portuguese company (Sociedade or LDA) for operational and tax efficiency. This is particularly relevant for hospitality developments that will generate commercial revenue.

## Professional Referrals

The agent should always redirect specific questions to professionals:
- Specific tax calculations: tax advisor / contabilista
- Legal structure recommendations: property lawyer / advogado
- Mortgage eligibility and rates: bank or mortgage broker
- Golden Visa eligibility: immigration lawyer
- Construction cost quotes: architect + quantity surveyor
- Environmental compliance: environmental consultant
- Structural integrity: civil engineer
