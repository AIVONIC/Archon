# Algarve ForSale - Planning & Permit Process

## How Development Permits Work in Portugal

The Burgau property has an existing build permit for spa, restaurant, and event space use. This is significant -- obtaining new hospitality permits in the Western Algarve is increasingly difficult due to planning restrictions, environmental protections (Costa Vicentina natural park proximity), and limited buildable land. A buyer inherits both the structure and the entitlement.

### Licenca de Construcao vs Licenca de Utilizacao

Licenca de Construcao (Building Permit) -- Permission to build or modify a structure. The Burgau property already has this. It authorises the construction that was completed in 2008.

Licenca de Utilizacao (Usage Permit) -- Permission to operate the building for its intended purpose. This is what a buyer needs to obtain after completing the fit-out. The existing building permit defines what uses are authorised (spa, restaurant, event space). The usage permit confirms the finished building meets the requirements for those uses.

## Typical Development Process: Shell to Operating Venue

### Phase 1: Due Diligence (1-2 months)

- Engage a Portuguese lawyer (advogado) specialising in property and planning law
- Verify the existing building permit status, conditions, and any time limitations with the Camara Municipal de Vila do Bispo (the local municipality)
- Commission a structural survey of the existing concrete structure
- Confirm utility connections: water, electricity, sewage, telecommunications
- Review any environmental restrictions related to proximity to protected areas
- Obtain a certidao predial (land registry certificate) and caderneta predial (tax office record)

### Phase 2: Project Design (2-4 months)

- Engage a licensed Portuguese architect (arquitecto) to develop the conversion project
- The architect submits the project to the Camara Municipal for approval
- The project must comply with the existing permit scope (spa, restaurant, event space) -- changes to use require a new licenciamento
- Interior layout, technical specifications, fire safety, accessibility compliance, and environmental requirements are all part of the submission
- If the project stays within the existing permit scope, approval is faster than a new application

### Phase 3: Construction (6-18 months depending on scope)

- Engage a licensed construction firm (empreiteiro) -- must be registered with INCI (Instituto da Construcao e do Imobiliario)
- Construction follows the approved project
- Municipal inspections at key stages
- Utility connections finalised during construction
- Interior fit-out: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, flooring, finishes, kitchen/spa equipment

### Phase 4: Licensing (1-3 months after construction)

- Final municipal inspection
- Fire safety certification
- Health and safety certification (for food/hospitality use)
- Application for Licenca de Utilizacao
- Tourism registration if operating as a hospitality venue (Turismo de Portugal)
- Once granted, the venue can legally operate

## Total Timeline

From purchase to operational venue: 12-24 months depending on project complexity, municipal processing speed, and construction scope. The Western Algarve municipalities tend to be thorough but not excessively slow. Having the existing permit and structure eliminates the longest phase of a typical development -- land acquisition, initial permitting, and structural construction.

## Who You Need on Your Team

### Advogado (Lawyer)
Handles purchase contract, due diligence, permit verification, and ongoing legal compliance. Should specialise in property and planning law in the Algarve. Budget: typically 1-2% of purchase price for conveyancing, plus hourly for additional work.

### Arquitecto (Architect)
Designs the conversion project and manages the municipal approval process. Must be registered with the Ordem dos Arquitectos. The architect is the project's interface with the municipality. Budget: typically 5-10% of construction cost.

### Empreiteiro (Construction Firm)
Executes the build. Must be INCI-registered. For a project of this scale, choose a firm with hospitality/commercial experience rather than residential builders. Budget: the largest cost -- see development cost estimates.

### Fiscal Representative
If the buyer is non-resident, a fiscal representative (representante fiscal) is required for tax purposes in Portugal.

### Project Manager (optional but recommended)
An independent project manager who coordinates between architect, builder, and municipality. Valuable for non-resident buyers who can't be on-site daily.

## Important Notes

- The existing permit's validity period should be verified -- permits can expire if construction hasn't commenced or been completed within the stipulated timeframe. The 2008 construction may satisfy this, but legal confirmation is essential.
- Any change to the permitted use (e.g., adding residential accommodation, changing from spa to hotel classification) requires a new licenciamento process, which is significantly longer.
- Environmental impact assessments may be required depending on the scope of the conversion and proximity to protected areas.
- All permit and planning questions should be discussed in detail during a viewing or with the seller's legal representative.
