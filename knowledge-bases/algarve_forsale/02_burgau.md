# Algarve ForSale - Burgau

## Overview

Burgau is a former fishing village on the western Algarve coast, in the municipality of Vila do Bispo. It has retained much of its traditional character -- whitewashed cottages, cobbled streets leading down to the beach, and active fishing boats still launching from the beach. The village sits within the Costa Vicentina Natural Park area, which protects the coastline from large-scale development.

## Character and Atmosphere

The village is known for its authentic Portuguese character. The old centre features narrow, winding streets with traditional whitewashed buildings. The more modern developments on the outskirts are tasteful and low-rise -- there are no high-rise buildings or large resort complexes. Historically known as "the smugglers' village" due to its hidden alleys and secluded coastline.

## The Beach

Praia de Burgau is a golden sand beach approximately 350 metres wide, sheltered between dramatic cliffs. It has a gentle slope making it suitable for families. The concrete boat launch in the centre of the beach reflects the village's continued fishing activity. The beach has a beach bar and is popular with both locals and visitors. The sheltered bay provides protection from the strong Atlantic winds that characterise the western Algarve coast.

## Location and Connectivity

- Distance from Lagos: approximately 20 minutes by car (10km)
- Distance from Faro Airport: approximately 1 hour by car
- Distance from Sagres: approximately 20 minutes west
- Distance from Portimao: approximately 35 minutes east
- Nearby villages: Salema (3 minutes), Praia da Luz (10 minutes)
- The Fisherman's Trail (Trilho dos Pescadores) runs along the coast and passes near Burgau
- Public bus connections exist but are limited; a car is recommended

## Nearby Attractions

- Ponta da Piedade, Lagos: spectacular cliff formations, 25 minutes
- Sagres Fortress and Cape St. Vincent (most southwestern point of Europe): 25 minutes
- Lagos historic centre, marina, and Mercado dos Escravos (first slave market museum in Europe): 20 minutes
- Costa Vicentina Natural Park: surrounding area, hiking and nature
- Forte de Sao Luis de Almadena: historical 17th-century fortress ruins between Burgau and Salema
- Multiple golf courses within 30 minutes
- Dinosaur footprints at Salema beach: 5 minutes

## Climate

- Mediterranean climate with Atlantic influence
- Average 300 days of sunshine per year
- Summer temperatures: 25-35C
- Winter temperatures: 10-17C
- Best visiting season: year-round, with peak tourism June-September
- Shoulder seasons (April-May, October-November) offer warm weather with fewer crowds
- Winter is quiet but mild -- many restaurants close seasonally

## Living in Burgau

The village has essential amenities: ATM, small supermarkets, pharmacies in nearby Salema, restaurants and bars, and a launderette. For larger shopping, medical services, or nightlife, Lagos is the reference point at 20 minutes. The international community is significant, particularly British, German, and Scandinavian residents. The area attracts surfers, nature lovers, retirees, and increasingly, remote workers and digital nomads.
