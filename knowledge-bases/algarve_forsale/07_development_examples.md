# Algarve ForSale - Development Examples & Comparables

## Why Shell Conversions Work in the Algarve

The Algarve has a track record of successful conversions from raw or semi-finished structures into high-value hospitality venues. The economics are driven by three factors: land and structure costs are a fraction of finished property values, the tourism market provides strong year-round demand, and the region's natural assets (climate, coastline, landscape) do the marketing work.

The Burgau property sits in the Western Algarve -- the less developed, more characterful end of the coast. This area has seen a particular surge in boutique hospitality: wellness retreats, surf lodges, eco-resorts, and creative residencies that attract a different demographic than the golf-and-resort market of the Central Algarve.

## Development Archetypes

### Wellness Retreat / Spa

Convert the structure into a wellness destination using the existing spa permit. Treatment rooms, yoga/movement studios, accommodation suites, restaurant serving health-focused cuisine, pool areas for hydrotherapy and relaxation.

The property has two pools, abundant natural water, 5,000m2 across three levels, and is 400m from the beach. The Western Algarve is already established as a wellness destination -- the area attracts yoga practitioners, surfers, and health-conscious travelers year-round. The climate supports outdoor wellness activities 300+ days per year.

Boutique wellness retreats in the Western Algarve typically charge EUR 150-400 per night depending on season and offering. Properties with established wellness programming, good reviews, and strong online presence achieve 65-80% occupancy across the full year, not just summer.

A 15-20 room wellness retreat at average EUR 200/night with 70% occupancy generates approximately EUR 750k-1M gross annual revenue.

### Boutique Hotel / Surf Lodge

Convert into a boutique accommodation with restaurant and event capability. Position around the surf culture of the Western Algarve -- Burgau, Sagres, and the wider Costa Vicentina are world-class surf destinations.

400m from the beach. Three levels allow separation of functions: accommodation, dining/social, and back-of-house/storage. The event space permit allows hosting surf competitions, film screenings, markets, and cultural programming that drives off-season bookings.

Boutique surf lodges in the Sagres/Western Algarve corridor achieve premium pricing during surf season (September-May, which is the opposite of typical tourism seasons -- surf is better in autumn/winter swells). Year-round positioning as both a surf destination and a general boutique hotel creates dual-season demand.

### Creative Hub / Co-Working Retreat

Convert into a hybrid space: co-working facilities, short/medium-term accommodation for remote workers and creatives, event space for workshops and conferences, restaurant/cafe as social anchor.

The Western Algarve has seen significant growth in the remote worker and digital nomad community. Aljezur, Lagos, and the surrounding area have active co-working and creative scenes. A 5,000m2 facility could combine private offices, shared workspaces, meeting rooms, accommodation, and event capability in one destination.

Co-living/co-working retreats in Portugal typically charge EUR 1,500-3,500 per month for combined accommodation and workspace, or EUR 50-100/day for short-stay desk access plus accommodation. The model generates revenue from multiple streams: workspace subscriptions, accommodation, events, F&B, and potentially membership programmes.

### Event Venue / Cultural Space

Maximise the event space permit. Convert into a multi-purpose venue for weddings, corporate retreats, music events, art exhibitions, and cultural programming. Accommodation for guests, restaurant for catering, and the pools/outdoor areas as event assets.

The Algarve is Portugal's premier destination wedding market. Venue hire for weddings in the Western Algarve ranges from EUR 5,000-25,000+ depending on the property and season. A venue that can host 200+ guests across indoor and outdoor spaces, with on-site accommodation and catering, commands premium pricing. The shoulder season (April-June, September-November) is particularly strong for events.

## Comparable Market Data

### Finished Hospitality Property Values

The Algarve average price per square metre reached EUR 3,467 in 2025. Finished hospitality properties command significantly higher prices per square metre than residential due to their revenue-generating potential.

Finished boutique hotels (10-30 rooms) in the Western Algarve have traded between EUR 1.5M and EUR 8M+ depending on location, condition, brand, and track record. Per-room values for established properties range from EUR 100,000-250,000.

Finished restaurants with permits in prime Western Algarve locations have traded between EUR 300,000 and EUR 1.5M depending on size, location, and licence value.

The key comparison: The Burgau property at EUR 600,000 for 5,000m2 represents EUR 120/m2. A finished hospitality property in the same area would trade at EUR 2,000-5,000/m2 or more. Even accounting for development costs of EUR 500-1,500/m2 for fit-out, the gap between total investment and finished market value represents significant development upside.

### The Western Algarve Premium

The Western Algarve (Vila do Bispo, Aljezur, western Lagos) commands a premium over the Central Algarve for boutique hospitality. The area's character -- undeveloped coastline, natural park protections, surf culture, smaller villages -- attracts a demographic that pays more for authenticity and exclusivity.

Properties in this corridor with established hospitality operations are rarely available because owners don't sell them -- the revenue is too good. When they do trade, prices reflect scarcity.
