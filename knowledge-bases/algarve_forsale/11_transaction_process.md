# Transaction Process - From Interest to Completion

## Overview

The agent guides buyers through the entire purchasing process. This is not a referral. The agent walks them through each step, explains what happens, what they need to prepare, and what to expect next.

## Step 1: Expression of Interest

What the buyer does: States they want to move forward, or asks "how do I buy this?"
What the agent does:
- Confirms their interest
- Asks for contact details (name, email, phone, country)
- Asks about their intended use and financing plan
- Explains the next steps clearly
- Forwards their details and offer to the seller's representative

What to say: "Great. Here is how the process works. First, I will connect you with the seller's representative who handles the formal side. They will reach out within 48 hours. In the meantime, let me walk you through what the buying process looks like in Portugal."

## Step 2: Reservation Agreement

What happens: Buyer signs a reservation agreement and pays a reservation deposit of EUR 5,000 to EUR 10,000. This takes the property off the market temporarily.
Timeline: Usually within 1-2 weeks of verbal agreement.
The deposit is deducted from the final price.

What the agent explains: "The reservation agreement secures your position while due diligence is completed. The deposit is typically EUR 5,000 to EUR 10,000 and is deducted from the purchase price at completion."

## Step 3: Due Diligence

What happens: Buyer's lawyer verifies legal status, permits, encumbrances, land registry. Buyer commissions structural survey. Takes 2-4 weeks.

What the buyer needs:
- A Portuguese lawyer (advogado). The agent can suggest that the buyer find one experienced in commercial property in the Algarve.
- A structural surveyor for the building inspection
- NIF (Portuguese tax number) if they do not have one yet
- Portuguese bank account (needed for completion)

What the agent explains: "During due diligence, your lawyer will verify the property title, check the existing permits, and confirm there are no encumbrances. A structural survey is recommended since this is a concrete shell. This typically takes two to four weeks."

## Step 4: Promissory Contract (CPCV)

What happens: Contrato de Promessa de Compra e Venda. Legally binding agreement between buyer and seller. Buyer pays 10-30% deposit (minus reservation amount already paid).
Key clause: If buyer pulls out, they lose the deposit. If seller pulls out, they pay double the deposit to the buyer.

What the agent explains: "The CPCV is the binding contract. You will pay a deposit of 10 to 30 percent, which is deducted from the final price. This contract protects both sides. If either party withdraws, there are financial consequences."

## Step 5: Completion (Escritura)

What happens: Final deed signed at a Portuguese notary (Cartorio Notarial). Full payment transferred. Property ownership changes hands. Registered at Conservatoria do Registo Predial.
Timeline: Usually 4-12 weeks after CPCV.

Costs at completion:
- IMT (property transfer tax): approximately 6.5% for commercial property at this value
- Stamp duty: 0.8%
- Notary and registration: EUR 500 to EUR 1,500
- Legal fees: 1-2% of purchase price

What the agent explains: "Completion happens at a notary. The full payment is transferred, the deed is signed, and ownership is registered. Total transaction costs including taxes and fees are typically 8-10% on top of the purchase price."

## Step 6: Post-Purchase

What the agent offers after sale:
- Guidance on the permitting process for development
- Connection to local professionals (architects, construction firms, project managers)
- Ongoing information about Burgau and the Algarve
- Updates on market developments relevant to their project

What the agent says: "Congratulations on your purchase. I am still here if you need information about the development process, local professionals, or anything about Burgau and the Algarve. Feel free to reach out anytime."

## Important Notes

- The agent does NOT handle money, contracts, or legal documents
- The agent guides, explains, and connects - the lawyer and notary handle the legal work
- All specific legal and tax advice should come from the buyer's own lawyer
- The agent should always recommend the buyer gets independent legal representation
